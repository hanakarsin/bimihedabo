Makueni governor Kivutha Kibwana and the county's senator Mutula Kilonzo has clashed again on social media in what appears to be an escalating political rift between them ahead of 2022.

Kivutha was re-elected in 2017 on Wiper party ticket but has since defected and turned a strong critic of the outfit and its leadership.

Buying-off opponents

In his tweet that attracted the attention of Mutula, the Makueni leader claimed Wiper's strength and the political supremacy of its leader Kalonzo Musyoka would be measured by the upcoming Machakos senatorial race and the Kitise/Kithuki ward by-election.

He alleged Kalonzo had sensed defeat and had resorted to buying-off opponents to avoid stiff competition.

"Wiper Movement. The Machakos senatorial and the Kitise/Kithuki ward (Makueni)by-elections will define Wiper's Hon. Stephen K.Musyoka's political future in (Ukambani) of 35 years. Trying to buy-off rival candidates or forcing provincial administration to support Wiper will not help," he tweeted.

Self-imposed chair

Reacting to the sentiments, Senator Mutula accused the Makueni county boss of imposing himself as Wiper party chairman and awarding himself a direct ticket.

"Says the person who imposed himself as chair of Wiper and negotiated a direct ticket in 2017 for being “ very popular “ !Mungu nifunze kunyamaza (sic)," he said.

The two leaders have been at loggerheads for a while with Kivutha accusing Mutula of seeking cheap popularity as he plans to succeed him in 2020.

About a week ago, the senator accused the governor of presiding over a failed administration that had done little to improve health care.

Mutula who was speaking at the burial function of his uncle, which was attended by Kibwana, lectured the county boss for failing to upgrade and modernise hospitals thus resulting in "avoidable deaths".

The tussle escalated to social media where Mutula accused the governor od "shedding crocodile tears" during his uncle burial

"You should have not bothered to attend his funeral. The crocodile tears were evident," he tweeted
