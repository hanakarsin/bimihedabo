Laikipia women representative, Cate Waruguru, has asked the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) to keep off the issue of allocating more constituencies.

The legislator faulted the electoral agency for opposing the increment of constituencies as proposed in the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) (Amendment) Bill.

She said the agency had no option but to wait for the National Assembly and the Senate to dispense off with the matter before it's given to them to draft a referendum question.

"We expect nothing more or less from what was proposed by Kenyans, so if they have any amendments they should be advised that it cannot be done now in the National Assembly or Senate," said Waruguru.

The IEBC through its chairman, Wafula Chebukati, told the Justice and Legal Committee that the BBI proponents had no powers to demarcate or allocate more constituencies, noting that those powers were vested with the agency only.

" It's in order for parliament or through a referendum to create additional constituencies, but the work of allocating them is work of IEBC," said Chebukati.

Waruguru, however, said that it is for that reason that Kenyans will be deciding on the same through a referendum.

The BBI process this week got a slight setback when the Justice committee said it will need 10 more days to iron out some critical issues.
