Former agriculture cabinet secretary Mwangi Kiunjuri has warned President Uhuru Kenyatta against extending his term in office.

Uhuru is expected to vacate office in 2022 when his second and last term ends.

Cling on power

But according to Kiunjuri, there is a plot by the head of state to cling to power through the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

The Service Party (TSP) leader said that he would move to the Supreme Court to seek advisory on the government's rumoured plan to extend its term in office being pegged on the BBI report.

He warned elected leaders to vacate office upon the end of their term.

''They must vacate office on August 9, 2022. There is no extension of the term of governors, MPs or the president. The Constitution is obvious, and very soon, we will move to the Supreme Court for advisory on the issues of the Constitution,'' said Kiunjuri.

Expanding the executive

The BBI report, which aims at expanding the Executive among other issues, continues to spur debate.

''There are other issues that are more important than BBI. What is important today is for the government to ensure the disburse money to counties so that governors can pay suppliers, contractors, contractors, and money to our pockets,'' explained Kiunjuri.

Kiunjuri who presided over a church fundraiser in Kangema, Murang'a county, on Saturday, June 26, said that some people are hiding behind the BBI report.

''But again, we need to remember that there are other issues that are more important than BBI. For example, civil servants must be paid a good time to cater to their families and educate their families. But now, this government has put priorities upside down,'' he explained.

He accused the ruling Jubilee administration of having a big appetite for foreign monetary loans saying that the monies were not properly utilised.

''The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has given us a lot of money to mention other money lending institutions, but we keep constructing roads and buildings, which is a good thing. But we are doing this, yet some people don't have food to put on the table,'' lamented Kiunjuri.

Lack of money

He said that some Kenyans lack money to pay school fees for their children and even give as an offering in places of worship.

"There are so many people in this country who don't have money to pay school fees for their children and cannot even give offerings to places of worship. So we need to first feed the population before prioritising other issues,'' he explained.

Although Uhuru has stated that it is time to hand over power to a different community, leaders from his Central Kenya backyard have a different about who will succeed him. Kiunjuri and his colleagues say that the GEMA community still has a role to play in the country's leadership.
