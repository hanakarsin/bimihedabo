Largest dog in the world

The biggest dog in the world, as the Guinness World Records 2018 book states, is called Freddy. He broke the previous record and became the tallest dog alive in the world. Freddy is a huge Great Dane, whose height is 1.035 m (3 feet 4.17 inches). This large hound lives in Leigh-on-Sea, Essex, together with his owner Claire Stoneman.

Claire speaks fondly of her gigantic pet, saying that she first got him a few weeks earlier than she should have because Freddy had problems with feeding off his mom. The Great Dane was a regular puppy, and there was no indication that he will ever grow so big. Claire definitely did not expect him to become huge, let alone the biggest dog in the world and the Guinness World Record breaker. Except for Freddy, Claire also owns another canine – his sister Fleur, even though she is not anywhere as huge as her brother.

Who is the biggest dog ever?

Freddy is not the tallest dog in history – he is only the tallest among currently living canines. The honorary title belongs to Zeus, who, like Freddy, was also the Great Dane. He was owned by Kevin Doorlag, the veterinary technician from Otsego, Michigan, who adopted the hound together with his wife, Denise. Zeus was 111.8 cm tall (3 feet 8 inches), and weighed 155 pounds. By the way, when Zeus stood on his hind legs, he was 223 cm (7 feet 4 inches) high! He appeared in the Guinness Book of World Records twice – in 2012 and 2013. The interesting fact is that the previous holder of the biggest dog title, Giant George, was also a Great Dane, and was only one inch shorter than his successor. Due to his sizes, Zeus had to consume 30 lbs of dog food per day. The owner of Zeus described his dog as a "gentle giant."

Zeus, the Great Dane, was adopted by the Doorlag family when he was only eight weeks old. They got the puppy from the couple who just had a litter. As Kevin Doorlag remembered, they instantly fell in love with Zeus. Eventually, he became a certified therapy animal, who attended hospitals and helped people by sitting on their laps. Kevin was thrilled to see people's reactions when Zeus interacted with them because it seemed like this puppy's purpose was to make everyone smile.

Kevin's wife Denise recalled the most bizarre questions connected with Zeus. People asked whether he was a horse, and where his saddle was. Generally, however, the couple was very grateful that people accepted and loved their pet so much, and acknowledged that he touched a lot of lives. Zeus had a popular page on Facebook with a large following, even though after his passing the owners took it down. He passed away at the young age of 5, just before his 6th birthday. The causes of death were natural, and Kevin announced that his pet was showing old age symptoms for quite some time. Even though Zeus is no longer in this world, his name is forever written in history as the largest dog that ever lived.

The heaviest dog ever

If we take weight into account instead of size, there was a Mastiff dog who weighed the whole 365 pounds! His name was Cloe, and he lived in the 1980s in Tibet. Even though Mastiffs are usually a large breed, the average weight among them is 190 pounds, which is nearly twice less than Cloe's weight. When this canine turned eight years old in 1989, his size was equal to the large donkey size.

Now that you know some unique puppy stories, it is time for the biggest dog rating. If you were planning to get a large canine, you can take a look at the list of the biggest dogs in the world and determine which of them catches your interest.

10. French Mastiff

On first sight, this dog breed might look intimidating because of their piercing eyes and serious expression. However, do not be fooled by the cover, because the French mastiff dogs, which are also known under the name "Bordeaux mastiff" or "Dogue de Bordeaux", are very affectionate, loving and loyal to their owner.

These dogs have a powerful and muscular body and are able to help out with pulling the dog carts and carrying heavy objects. They also have highly developed guarding abilities. Long ago, the French mastiffs were guarding the castles that belonged to European nobles. The dogs of this breed usually weigh up to 140 pounds, and their height reaches up to 26 inches.

9. Anatolian Shepherd

This dog breed originates from ancient Turkey and is alternatively called "Karabash". Usually, large dog breeds have a shorter lifespan than small ones, but this one is an exception: one of the reasons why you should consider getting an Anatolian shepherd is that the average dog of this breed lives around 13-15 years.

You should keep in mind, however, that the Anatolian shepherds are not very good for families with children, because they can demonstrate some aggressive behaviour. Usually, these dogs are used for guard purposes: they protect livestock and possess a very high speed that allows them to track down various predators. The average Anatolian shepherd's weight is 140 pounds, and they can reach 32 inches when it comes to height.

8. Newfoundland

This breed is very large and has a great capacity to learn. Originally, the Newfoundland puppies were bred for assisting Canadian fishers with their work, but later they started performing many more tasks and even acting like life saviours.

There is a popular story circulating about the Newfoundland dog Rigel saving people in one of the lifeboats on the sinking ship Titanic, and making sure they got noticed by the rescue team from the Carpathia ship. Also, there is a very famous fictional dog of this breed – her name is Nana, and she is from the Peter Pan play. In this work by J.M. Barrie, she watches over children. Newfoundland dogs truly make wonderful pets and companions. Their weight can reach up to 150 pounds, while their height is 29 inches average.

7. Neapolitan Mastiff

The dogs of this breed normally possess a high level of intelligence and independence. Despite what you might think, they are actually very quiet. However, their guard skills are exceptional: they are incredibly protective of their owners. Their appearance is quite fearsome, and the owners of large properties often obtain these dogs for protection purposes. Just like the other Mastiff dogs, this one is quite large: its height at the shoulders is 31 inches, and they weigh up to 150 pounds.

6. Leonberger

This breed is one of the biggest ones, and also one of the noblest and beautiful. They are also very affectionate and passionate, like the Newfoundlands, only they can reach even bigger sizes. The Leonbergers have a double coat, muscular body, and intelligent look in their eyes. Every person who loves large and smart dogs would enjoy having a Leonberger at home. Quite often, they serve as search and rescue dogs and are successful in this job. The average weight of a male dog of this breed is 170 pounds, and they can also be around 31 inches tall.

5. Irish wolfhound

As it can be seen from the name, the Irish wolfhound breed originally came from Ireland and was used for hunting the wolves. Even though the Irish wolfhound dogs have a harsh background, they are very sociable (if you work with them properly) and adapt perfectly to family life. They are one of the tallest dog breeds and are suited for the countryside rather than the urban way of life. They usually reach 180 pounds in height with their enormously long legs, and their weight is averagely 32 inches.

4. Great Dane

There is a reason why this breed had so many world record breakers, the impressive stories of which you have read above. The Great Danes are very easy-going and full of joy, but being an owner of such a sizable dog is a huge commitment. The hounds of this breed are very often fondly called "the Apollo of dogs."

They are sweet and loving, but despite this, they are also excellent at guarding homes and scaring off intruders. By the way, probably the most popular fictional Great Dane, who also showed the amazing nature of this breed to people of all ages, is none other than the famous cartoon character Scooby-Doo. An average height of a Great Dane is 32-34 inches, and weight fluctuates between 150 and 180 pounds.

3. Saint Bernard

The St. Bernard dogs are very loyal, friendly, affectionate, and always drooling. They received their name after the hospice St. Bernard, where they were originally helping out with comforting injured people.

2. Caucasian Shepherd

This breed, also known as "Ovcharka," originally came from the Caucasian mountains and have gained popularity among the lovers of large dogs. They are gorgeous pets who have a loyal look in their eyes and can give the best hugs due to their fluffy coat. Without discipline and love, they can get aggressive, especially with the other canines – no wonder this breed is frequently used to guard livestock. If you are an experienced dog owner who can get along with such a shepherd, then it will make your nights a little warmer. 1. English Mastiff

A beautiful English Mastiff is a dream of everyone who always wanted to get themselves a large furry companion. Not a single burglar will consider invading the home of the English Mastiff owner.
