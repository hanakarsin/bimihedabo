Brenda Nantongo, General Katumba Wamala's deceased daughter, will be buried in Kampala on Thursday, June 3, in Kikandwa village, Uganda.

Brenda's mother, Catherine Katumba, said her 32-year-old daughter's body would be put in a gown because she looked forward to a wedding.

She further stated that they had brought up the subject of marriage multiple times during informal conversations.

“I do not usually speak at functions, but I had committed to speak at Brenda’s wedding and she would say ‘I would wear a gown of this make," said Brenda's mother.

"She would say ‘I don’t like a certain make’ and, so, we knew exactly what she would wear [on her wedding day],” she added.

According to media sources, Brenda will be buried in a sleeveless, round-necked cream American satin gown with white pearls on the breast and a cocktail hat fastened to her hair.

Speaking at a funeral service on Wednesday, June 2, Brenda's mother added that she decided her daughter had to wear a gown to mark her last celebrations.

"Even if she is dead, let her wear it in a coffin, let us throw this wedding as her last celebration," she said.

Indeed, Brenda’s body in the glittering hard-wood casket at their urban home in Bulabira was dressed in a sleeveless and round-necked cream American satin gown.

Makeup was visible on the face, the eyebrows were drawn, and the eyelashes were darkened with mascara while red lipstick textured her lips.
