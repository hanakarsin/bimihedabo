Pope Francis Speaks About Corruption And Tribalism

April 18, 2024
Post

After presiding over mass at St Joseph The Worker Catholic Church in Kangemi, the Pope went to Safaricom Stadium, Kasarani to address the youth. In attendance was President Uhuru Kenyatta and First Lady Margaret Kenyatta.

The Pope has often expressed his passion for the youth and that is why he set aside time specifically for them. After the address, he went on to address Catholic bishops and clergy at the same venue.

President Uhuru Kenyatta was among hundreds of government officials and thousands of Kenyans who were at Kasarani Stadium waiting to listen to the Pope's address to the youth. The message centred mainly on the need to stamp out corruption, radicalisation and tribalism. It is estimated that the stadium had nearly 100,000 people.

Pope Francis has very high regard for the youth who are a symbol of any nation's future. His message has also centred on how best to grant them opportunities in sport, education, access to health and employment.

The youth are faced with many challenges including lack of access to education and employment opportunities, drugs and sexual abuse as well as radicalisation. Many youths in Kenya and all over the world have also become an easy target for recruitment into terror related activities. This is something that has concerned Pope Francis, who called for the implementation of initiatives aimed at empowering the youth and safeguarding their future.

See below what the Pope says:

12 noon: Above all, I ask you, please pray for me. I also need prayers.

11:47 am: If a young woman or man has no work, cannot study, what can he or she do? Lead a life of delinquency, a life of drug abuse or even commit suicide?

The first thing to stop a young person being recruited is education and work. If a young person has no work, then what future awaits him or her? That’s where being recruited into radicalisation comes in.

11:44 am: Why do young people full of ideals allow themselves to be recruited to radicalisation? They leave their lives behind in order to learn how to kill? This is a question all of you must pose to all people in authority?

11:42 am: What can we do to stop young people being recruited to radicalisation?

What can we do to try to win them back after they have been recruited and are trying to come back?

11:40 am: Corruption is not a path to life. It’s a path to death.

11:36 am: Corrupt people don’t live in peace.

11:35 am: If you don’t want corruption in your heart and your country, then start now. If you don’t start the journey towards ending corruption, the person next to you won’t start.

11:34 am: Also, in the Vatican, there are cases of corruption. Corruption is something that eats from inside. And then, we end up in a poor way. Each time we accept a bribe, and we put it in our pockets, we destroy our hearts, we destroy our personalities and we destroy our country. Please, don’t develop that taste for that sugar which is called corruption.

11:30 am: Corruption-Can we justify corruption? Just for the mere fact that everyone is corrupt? How can we be Christians and overcome this evil of corruption.

11:28 am: Let’s stand up and raise our hands against tribalism. We are one nation. Tribalism isn’t just raising our hands today but an expression of our hearts. To overcome this tendency of tribalism is a daily endeavour.

11:24 am: Tribalism-It can destroy. It can mean your hands hidden behind your back and having a stone in each hand to throw to others.

11:23 am: Do you want to overcome challenges or be overcome by them?

11:20: Young people, we don’t live in heaven but on earth. And, earth is full of difficulties, not only the opportunities and sometimes invitations that will lead you astray to evil. But, we have something big-the capacity to choose. Which path do you want to choose? To choose the path of difficulties or of opportunities?

Pope Francis will end his three-day visit to Kenya on Friday afternoon. At approximately 3:30 pm, the Pope and his delegation will leave for a three day visit to Uganda and later conclude his Africa tour in the Central African Republic.
