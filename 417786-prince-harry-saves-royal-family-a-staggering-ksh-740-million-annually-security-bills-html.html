It has emerged that the Royal family is saving a whopping KSh 740 million yearly on security bills that could have been channeled towards the protection and safety of Prince Harry.

According to media reports, the saving comes after Prince Charles decided to cut off security for the embattled Harry.

Express reported that Harry had to adjust to paying for his own security and entourage after Prince Charles stopped funding him and his wife, Meghan Markle, last summer.

Prince Harry now pays for his own security and entourage bills

The English website reported that Harry’s security and entourage bills are now being footed by the Duke of Sussex himself as Charles abruptly stopped providing his son with financial support. A Royal finance expert said the Duke would have to "generate a lot of income" to cover the total costs of the bills.

According to the publication, David McClure spoke to the ITV documentary Harry and William: What Went Wrong?. Express quotes McClure:

"One of the biggest things Harry had to give up when he stopped being a working royal was someone paying for his security. Now it's been estimated that his security bill every year is about KSh 448 million.''"In addition to that, he has his big entourage of people - he has lawyers, he has managers, he has public relations people. The total bill for that entourage has been estimated at about KSh 747 million a year. Now that means he has to generate a lot of income."
