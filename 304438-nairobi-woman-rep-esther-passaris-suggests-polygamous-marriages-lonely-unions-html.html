Nairobi Woman Rep Esther Passaris suggests polygamous marriages like hers are lonely unions

Post

Nairobi Woman Representative Esther Passaris recently shared an interesting perspective on polygamous marriages even as the debate on such arrangements rages on in the country.

There is no denying the 53-year-old is still stunning despite her age and for this reason, it usually comes as a surprise to many when they learn of her marital status.

It is quite uncommon for Passaris to publisise her relationships and the fact that she is in a polygamous marriage may be one of the reasons.

The mother of two was forced to issue a rare statement on her relationship status after a curious online user pressed her on the matter.

It all spanned from a video she shared on Twitter while she was in an airport in Porto, Portugal where she talked about her struggles with fibroids among other things.

However, the men following the thread could hardly keep up with her very informative talk and they quickly switched attention to whether she was single or not.

In response, the lovely woman rep courteously responded by clarifying that she was indeed married for a solid 26 years.

“I haven't been 'single' for the last 26 years,” she responded.

Passaris went on to give some solid truths about polygamous marriages, particularly for the ladies in them.

“When one is in a polygamous marriage, she can attest and relate to singlehood based on the solitude lifestyle. When one is headstrong and not always submissive, she relates to being more single than married,” she added.

Because polygamy is legal in Kenya, it has attracted both endorsements and criticism from the public in equal measure.

Several political figures in polygamous marriages include Bungoma Senator Moses Wetangula, and former Garsen MP Danson Mungatana.
