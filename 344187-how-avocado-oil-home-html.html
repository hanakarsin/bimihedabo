How to make avocado oil at home

May 17, 2024
Post

Nowadays, avocado oil available on the supermarket shelves is so expensive. If you fancy the product, then its pricing should not deter you from using it. This is especially true as you can churn it out on your own. What steps should you follow to make avocado oil at home?

Making a DIY avocado oil doesn’t require any certificates or technical knowledge. With this guide, the process is as simple as ABC! Here is everything you need to know on how to extract avocado oil at home and much more.

How to make avocado oil

This product is made by extracting oil from ripe avocados. Depending on the type of homemade oil extractor you use, you might be left with some smashed avocado, which is perfect for baking or even making guacamole!

Method 1: Extracting oil by cooking

You will need the following:

  • 12 ripe avocados
  • A knife
  • Blender
  • A cooking pot/pan
  • Oven or any other source of heat
  • A bowl
  • A spatula
  • A cheesecloth
  • A clean bottle to put the extracted oil

Stick to the steps below:

  • Wash the fruits: For hygiene, start by cleaning them on running water one by one.
  • Peel the avocadoes: Cut them into half using a knife. Use your spatula to scoop out the edible part. Deposit it in a blender or a food processor.
  • Blend the fruits: Turn on your blender, choose the right speed and process the avocados till they form a smooth uniform puree. Turn off the food processor and scoop all its contents into a pot or pan.
  • Cook on moderate heat: Turn your cooker or oven on to heat the fruits in the pot. Keep stirring after every five or so minutes. When the puree boils, you should be able to observe bubbling.
  • Cook till the blend gets dark: Keep cooking and also stirring after every five minutes. The colour of the puree will eventually change from green to something dark-brownish. The water will have evaporated.
  • Transfer the concoction into your bowl: When the fruits change to the stated colour, stop cooking and spoon it into a clean pan.
  • Use a cheesecloth: Place it on top of your bowl, then holding it in place, turn the pudding basin upside down. Hold the cloth together such that you are only left with the avocado blend.
  • Extracting oil: Squeeze the cheesecloth to strain its contents. You should do so on top of a bowl to avoid spillage. Keep changing the position of your grip and squeeze hard until no more lard comes out.
  • Store the oil: Finally, pour your bowl’s contents into a clean bottle with a lid.

    • Hooray! You have successfully made your avocado oil. It is now ready for use.

    Method 2: Extraction by applying pressure

    You will need the following:

    • 12 ripe avocados
    • An orange press
    • A bowl
    • A coffee filter
    • A spatula
    • A clean bottle to pour in the made oil

    You should adhere to the following steps:

  • Wash the avocados thoroughly: Start by cleaning the fruits for hygienic purposes. Remember that you will be squeezing it to extract the much-needed oil.
  • Slice the avocados: Use your knife to divide all of them into two equal parts. Dispose of the seeds and use a spatula to scoop the edible part. Set it aside for use in other guacamole recipes.
  • Place the skins in the orange press: Take all your avocado coatings and stack them in the hand machine on top of each other. An orange press is designed to extract juice from oranges and other citrus fruits, and hence it is also the best manual machinery you can use to press your oil out of the avocados.
  • Squeeze the skins: Pull the orange press lever down with force until the machine’s millet touches the fruits. Your mallet should press the avocados together so that the oil is wrung out to a reservoir.
  • Press several times: The first push might not yield a lot of drops. However, with repeated motion, you can obtain the right amount of oil.
  • Strain the lard: You will see chunks of avocados in your end product. Use a coffee filter to separate them from the useful produce. It will trap all the unwanted pieces that might have exited the orange press with your oil.
  • Bottle it: Keep your product in the strainer for an overnight. It will allow the oil sticking to the avocado chunks to filter out slowly. Pour the end product in a clean bottle and use a cork to seal it.

    Method 3: Extraction by sieving

    You will need the following:

    • 12 ripe avocados
    • A blender
    • A knife
    • A baking tray
    • Oven
    • A spatula
    • Square gauze or a cotton cloth
    • A bowl
    • A bottle

    The following procedure should be used:

  • Wash and rinse your avocadoes: Use clean running water to do so.
  • Scoop out the fruit part: Slice your fruit using a knife into two equal parts. Dispose of the seed and any other unwanted part. Use a spatula to spoon out all your twelve avocadoes.
  • Blend the mixture: Once you have scooped out all the fruits, transfer them into a food processor. You should puree them until a thick uniform paste is left.
  • Transfer them into a baking tray: Switch off your food processor and spoon out all its content into a baking pan. Use a spatula to make sure the paste is evenly spread over the salver.
  • Place the content into an oven: After evenly spreading your fruits, place the tray into your oven. Set the temperature to be slightly above 50 degrees celsius. Remember that you are not baking the contents but rather drying them.
  • Keep the mixture in moderate heat for 5 hours: Let your contents to dry out in the oven for close to 5 hours. Check the mixture after every 1 hour to make sure it isn’t burning. Once ready, it should have a dark brown-green colour.
  • Scoop the contents: After 5 hours, take your tray out of the oven. Use a bigger spatula to scratch the fruits out of the pan. Place them into a square gauze or a cotton cloth.
  • Extract the oil: Squeeze your sack containing the avocadoes on top of a large bowl. Pinch the corners together with force. Readjust your hands so that all the contents are pressurized to exact the much-needed oil. Stop mangling once nothing yields.
  • Store the oil: Pour all the collected droplets into a clean, clear bottle and seal it, Dispose of the dried avocadoes.
