Higher education is essential, especially in a country like Kenya where jobs are not always easy to come by. One such institution in Kenya is the Kitale National Polytechnic (KNP).

It offers a wide range of courses, from business related, agriculture-related, engineering related, math, sciences, among others. The courses offered range from diploma to certificate, professional courses, short courses, and trade and crafts courses. About Kitale National Polytechnic

Before checking out the courses and fee structure, here are some things you need to know about the institution:

History

Kitale National Polytechnic was started in 1980 as a public institution (with funding assistance from the Swedish International Development Agency). It was converted into a technical training institute in 1986 (then known as Kitale Technical School). Initially, it started by offering artisan and KASNEB ACNC (Accounts Clerk National Certificate) courses. Craft courses in engineering and building, as well as CPA, were introduced into the institution. Later the institute started offering diploma courses in a range of disciplines. The school was upgraded to Polytechnic status in 2016, hence changing to its current name.

In the 30 plus years, the institute has been operational, it has grown to be one of the top technical institutes/polytechnics in the country. It has also seen a significant increase in the courses offered, resulting in tremendous growth in enrolment numbers. The current enrolment for the school stands at 4,900 students.
