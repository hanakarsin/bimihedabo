CPA course requirements, colleges and schools in Kenya

February 13, 2024
Post

The Certified Public Accountant (CPA) examination is held twice in June and December. CPA is grouped into three parts each with a further two sections. This means for one to be a CPA holder he or she would have to finish all six sections. Students have the liberty to attempt two sections of a part in one sitting or separately in ascending order. Proceeding to the next level is only allowed after the lower sections have been passed. The good thing with the CPA course is that one can get a job even with a few sections completed thus empowering individuals financially as they cater for their needs to complete the remaining parts.

The big question here is what does it take for one to qualify as a CPA student in Kenya? To begin with, a student must meet the minimum requirements for this course to stand a chance of being accepted. Even with the qualifications, the first step should be students taking the initiative to assess their interest in the profession and the ability to cope with the problematic conditions one has to go through in the course. The course demands a good understanding of mathematical computations, analytical and communication skills if one is to pass in any of the examinations.

CPA course minimum entry requirements

The minimum entry requirements for a CPA course in Kenya are as follows:

  • KCSE aggregate C+ (Plus) and at least a C+ in Mathematics and English.
  • Alternatively, Kenya Advanced Certificate of Education (KACE) with at least two principle passes and credits in mathematics and English in the Kenya Certificate of Education (KCE) level, or
  • An Accounting Technicians Certificate (ATC) or the Accounts Clerk National Certificate (ACNC), or
  • A degree from a recognised and reputable university, or
  • Any other relevant certificates or Diplomas as may be approved by the Board of KASNEB.

CPA colleges and schools in Kenya

Many schools and colleges offer CPA in Kenya. Here are some of the most popular institutions you can enrol in if you are looking to pursue to the course:

KCA University

KCA University is located along Thika Road with other campuses in Nairobi town and Kisumu. The school aims to produce students with management, financial management, and information technology skills that are much needed in the modern job market. If offers all CPA modules.

Kibabii University

Kibabii University offers the CPA certificate under KASNEB at the Bungoma Town Centre on a part-time basis. The fee is Kshs. 15,000 for two sections.

Dominion Training Institute

The Dominion Training Institute is a technical vocational college established in 2004 to offer tuition in accounting, management, ICT, Journalism and community development among other courses.

Strathmore University

Strathmore University acknowledges that a course in accountancy is relevant no matter what career learners choose to pursue.

Nairobi Institute of Business Studies

NIBS offers KASNEB examinations including a Certified Public Account course at the main campus. The school’s enrolment deadlines are in February and August.

Zetech University

Zetech University has established itself as one of the best accounting schools in the country. Part of the story behind the reputation is the school’s commitment to offering market-oriented CPA courses.

Vision Institute of Professionals

Vision Institute of Professionals is one of the best accounting schools in the country. All its six campuses offer KASNEB courses including CPA. The fees charged at this institution are pocket-friendly thereby attracting many students.

Summit Institute of Professionals

Summit Institute of Professionals is a centre of excellence found within Nairobi’s CBD. It has impressive facilities and dedicated lecturers well versed with the field of accounting.
