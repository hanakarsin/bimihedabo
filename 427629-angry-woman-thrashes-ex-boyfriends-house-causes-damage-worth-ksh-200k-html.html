A woman has been arraigned for breaking into her ex-partner's home and destroying property to the tune of £2,000 which is an equivalent of at least KSh 302,569.

Lauren Blaney, who was in the company of a man, were reported to the police by a member of the public who saw them smashing a window at the home of her ex-lover identified as James Campbell. The incident allegedly happened at 8pm on March 21, 2021.

The Mirror reported that the woman was seen smashing Campbell's window sing a guitar.

“There were spots of blood on shattered glass and blood splatters around the address,” Nemat said.

Campbell took away children

Police also found items, including food, in a rucksack Blaney had been seen with.

Other properties damaged included a guitar, two television sets, ornaments, and clothing.

The guitar's estimated worth is KSh 75,681 (£500) was also missing.

The court also heard that Campbell, 48, was out at the time the woman raided his house.

He stated that the attack left him scarred and less confident, forcing him to stay at a friend's house.

Campbell said he had been focussing on taking care of his daughters after breaking up with Blaney.

Blaney in her defence said Campbell took away her children which made her (Blaney) so angry that she got herself drunk and decided to damage her ex's house.

The court heard Blaney had six previous convictions for 13 offences, including theft.

Rachel Oakdene, while defending Blaney, said a pre-sentence report revealed Blaney’s chaotic lifestyle, but there was hope she could put that behind her.

"She had made a complete confession to police and pleaded guilty at the first opportunity," Oakdene said.

She added that Blaney was receiving drug and alcohol misuse treatment and hoped to get her children back next year.

Blaney pleaded guilty to burglary, and the court heard her partner was charged with criminal damage and assaulting an emergency worker and fined by magistrates.
