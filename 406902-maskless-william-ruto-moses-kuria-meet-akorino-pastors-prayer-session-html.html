Deputy President William Ruto and Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria were earlier today, Thursday, March 25, spotted with a section of Akorino pastors.

The two leaders were joined by Runyenjes MP Eric Muchangi for a prayer session and what they also called a consultative meeting with the leadership of the Akorino faith led by Archbishop Kinyanjui.

The Gatundu South parliamentarian shared photos from the meeting and but many Kenyans were quick to call out Ruto and the lawmaker for failing to wear their masks especially at a time when the third wave of COVID-19 is rocking the country.

A couple of weeks ago, Ruto announced the suspension of all his public engagements citing concerns about the surge of COVID-19 cases in the country.

His announcement came hours after President Uhuru Kenyatta announced new measures for containing the spread of the coronavirus in Kenya.

"The COVID-19 infection rates have become a serious source of concern making it necessary for GoK to take remedial measures to manage and contain the pandemic," wrote the DP on March 12.
