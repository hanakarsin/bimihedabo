Deputy President William Ruto has pledged to allocate hustlers KSh 100 million every year after he becomes president in 2022.

The DP, who has been championing for a bottom-up approach method of governance using his hustler nation said it was time to shift focus to small-business owners instead of the politicians at the top.

Speaking to Mtwapa residents on Sunday, February 7, Ruto said just like the Jubilee government allocates KSh 100 million for development to every constituency, he will similarly ensure hustlers get the same amount for their enterprises once he rises to the top seat.

"Just like we allocate KSh 100 million to every constituency in every year for development projects, this time round, we will allocate KSh 100 million every year, government funds, to help people like mama mboga, those that use wheelbarrows and carts," he said.

Ruto's hustler nation has seen him earn praise and criticism in equal measure as 2022 succession politics gain momentum.

President Uhuru Kenyatta is the latest politician to dismiss Ruto's narrative during a meeting with politicians from Mt Kenya at the Sagana State Lodge.

The president told off Ruto over his wheelbarrow campaign stating that the youngsters needed jobs and not the one-wheel invention.

While painting his deputy as a dishonest man who should not be trusted, Uhuru took issue with Ruto's opposition to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) which according to him, will be the youths' springboard to a brighter future.

"Then you (Ruto) come here and pose as if you are the one that knows it all. You claim that you understand the problems of the youth. You tell them that you will give them wheelbarrows. Who told you the youth want wheelbarrow? These people want jobs. They want to be independent," Uhuru said.
