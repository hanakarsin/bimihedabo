You Have Betrayed Kenyans- CORD Tells Uhuru On Uganda Sugar Deal

June 28, 2024
Post

The war of words regarding the Uganda sugar deal that Uhuru is said to have signed while on a three-day visit of Uganda took a new twist after opposition accused the president of betraying Kenyans.

In a statement to newsrooms, Coalition for Reforms and Democracy (CORD) said that the deal- which will allow Uganda to export sugar into Kenya- is bound to criple the local sugar industry.

On Wednesday, August 12, 2015, Uhuru Kenyatta had blasted CORD leader Raila Odinga for lying to Kenyans that the deal would result in an influx of cheap sugar into the country.

But CORD maintained that the Uganda Sugar deal is being trivialised yet it has far reaching consequences to the economy of sugar cane growing zones.

READ ALSO: Why Raila Doubts Uhuru’s Uganda Deal

CORD holds that unscrupulous traders will take advantage of the deal to import cheap sugar into Kenya through Uganda.

Under the East African Customs Management Act, sugar produce in Uganda or Kenya is supposed to trade freely without restrictions.

But Kenya imposed a ban on Uganda sugar in 2012 following accusations that Uganda traders were shipping cheap illegal sugar into Kenya thus distorting the local market.

The deal has raised a political storm with political leaders from sugar growing zones accusing the president of entering a deal that will hurt their constituents.

A meeting that was to take place between Uhuru and Western MPs on Thursday, August 13, 2015 over the Uganda sugar deal flopped as they claimed it was not well represented.
