Newspapers review for September 30: Kenyans to secure licences before holding harambees if new bill

July 15, 2024
Post

As the demonetisation process comes to its close, newspapers on Monday, September 30 pointed to a possibility of CBK not striking its target of collecting KSh 217 billion in old KSh 1,000 notes.

The dailies also gave special interest to the heated race ahead of Kibra by-election and deputy president William Ruto's prediction of doom for Raila Odinga's Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

1. The Star

The Star reported of a new bill set to be tabled in Parliament which if passed into law, will require any one who wants to convene harambees to obtain a licence.

The bill is sponsored by Ndaragwa MP Jeremiah Kioni and it visions to provide legal frameworks for the harambee culture that has existed in the country since 1963.

It proposes that a person will have to inform county officials that they intend to solicit funds from the public at least 21 days before the drive.

Contributors will be required to declare the source of their money and the same must be reflected while filing their annual tax returns.

Earlier, Suba South MP John Mbadi had proposed that politician's contributions in harambee's be limited at KSh 100,000; a move he claimed will help stem money laundering.

2. The Standard

The daily reported on a case of a 29-year-old man who sued his father for not paying for his master's degree program.

Allan Njau Waiyaki told the court that his father was a rich man and he wanted him to pay him KSh 2.8 million as he pursued his degree in terrorism and security studies.

However, Justice Aggrey Muchelule dismissed the case and slapped the young man with costs.

According to Justice Muchelule, the petitioner already had a job as a director of a petroleum company which gave him a decent income and his father's parental responsibility ceased when he crossed the 18-year mark.

The judge also observed that Eddie Waiyaki Hinga had paid for his son's undergraduate studies.

"The respondent may be a rich father. However, in the circumstances of this case, he had no obligation to pay for the appellant's masters degree program," he ruled.

3. Taifa Leo

Amani National Congress (ANC) leader Musalia Mudavadi has said plans are afoot to establish a new NASA alliance that will be identified as Kenyan National Super Alliance.

According to Mudavadi, the current coalition which has been met with a lot of turbulence, was never a vessel its partners could use to clinch power but it was a vision of uniting Kenyans.

His sentiments came at a time when the ODM Party has asked Mudavadi to come out clear on whether or not he is interested in remaining in NASA.

This contest follows the Kibra by-election race where the moribund coalition partners; ODM, ANC and Ford Kenya have fielded candidates. The ODM Party feels betrayed by its partners.

4. People Daily

A section of politicians allied to Jubilee Party have dismissed claims that the Kibra by-election is a political contest between DP Ruto and Opposition Chief Raila.

Led by Lang'ata MP Nixon Korir, the leaders slammed the narrative that Mariga was Ruto's project stating that the footballer was a Jubilee candidate who was also endorsed by party leader President Uhuru Kenyatta.

They also took a swipe at Raila who has been referring to Kibra as his political bedroom stating that it was at the electorate's disposal to decide who will become MP.

"The perception being created that this contest is between DP Ruto and Raila as part of a warm-up duel in 2022 to succeed President Uhuru is far-fetched. This is simply a contest between aspiring parliamentarians," said Korir.

5. Daily Nation.

The ODM party has said that it will not participate in any talks that will lead to fronting of ANC leader Musalia Mudavadi as a compromise candidate ahead of the 2022 General Election.

According to ODM party Secretary General Edwin Sifuna, Mudavadi had on several occasions shown signs that he could not be trusted.

"We (ODM) as one of the big players are ruling out involvement in such discussion because Mudavadi has demonstrated more than once that he is not a trusted ally," said Sifuna.

This comes even as talk is rife that Mudavadi could be fronted as a safe pair of hands. However, the ANC Party maintains there are no such plans.
