Detectives drawn from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) are investigating a former Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) officer for allegedly forging military training calling letters.

Working with military police officers, the sleuths pounced on one Washington Mutinda Mutuku at a Mlolongo resort, Machakos county on Wednesday, February 17.

According to preliminary police reports, Mutinda masqueraded as a senior KDF commander, whiled away time at the resort waiting to con and strike a deal with unsuspecting victims.

The officers recovered two forged calling letters for the ongoing KDF recruitment, and KSh 180,000 believed to a bribe.

"Investigations have since been initiated to verify the authenticity of the calling letters, their source and other accomplices that may be involved in the malpractice," the DCI stated.

The detectives cautioned members of the public to be wary of tricksters who were on the prowl looking to swindle money from them in the ongoing military and police recruitment exercises.

"Both giving and taking bribe are illegalities that will be met with the full force of the law," the DCI said.

Fake letters

The KDF recently raised concerns over increasing cases of innocent Kenyans being conned by fraudsters who have been peddling around calling letters into the service.

The military department has distanced itself from those letters and warned members of the public against falling victim to such invitations as they were not genuine.

In a statement on Saturday, January 30, KDF shared samples of some of the letters that had been sent to 'recruits' in advance and warned such documents would be rejected and legal action taken against those in possession.

"Be warned that criminals are selling fake KDF recruitment calling letters. This information is to enlighten and deter Kenyans from being cheated. KDF recruitment is free and fair," read the statement.

Recruitment drive

The month-long KDF recruitment drive began on February 8 and will run up to February 19.

The KDF announced positions for the General Service Officer (GSO) cadets, specialist officers, general duty recruits, tradesmen and women. Kenyans looking to join the forces as constables will also be recruited.

The prospective candidates must be Kenyan citizen with no dual citizenship and have a valid national identity card.
