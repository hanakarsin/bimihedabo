Newspapers Review For July 5: CS Fred Matiang'i to Lock Horns with William Ruto in 2022 Presidential

On Monday, July 5, the dailies reported on the verbal duel between Deputy President William Ruto and former premier Raila Odinga.

The dailies also highlighted the possibility of Interior CS Fred Matiang'i being groomed to face Ruto in the next year's presidential race.

1. The Star

According to this publication, Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i might face off with Ruto in the next year's polls.

The Star reports that Matiang'i is working with Raila on an elaborate plan by uniting Nyanza to vote as a bloc.

The CS is considered astute and also the first among equals in President Uhuru Kenyatta's cabinet. He is believed to have replaced the deputy president in overseeing Uhuru’s projects.

Due to his role in government, Matiang'i is treated as the senior-most state operative in the Nyanza region and can easily mobilise leaders to a roundtable.

Therefore, he is seen as a befitting candidate capable of trouncing the deputy president who is passionate about succeeding his boss.

2. Daily Nation

The daily focussed its radar on the barbs witnessed between ODM supremo Raila Odinga and his political nemesis, Willaim Ruto.

Over the weekend, the two leaders unleashed attacks on each other over graft claims.

It all began when Raila stated that his administration would crack down on corrupt leaders and push for their imprisonment.

In his response, Ruto said Raila was not clean, accusing him of overseeing a corruption cartel responsible for the multi-million Kemsa scandal and the Kazi Kwa Vijana programme that stalled during the grand-coalition government.

Raila wondered why Ruto would be uneasy whenever discussions about corruption are brought forth even without his name being mentioned.

The ODM chief urged Ruto to resign now that he had decided to be an outsider in the government he is part of.

3. People Daily

People Daily also had space for the barbs between Raila and Ruto regarding the war on graft.

The former premier had stated that should he succeed Uhuru as the fifth president, his government would embark on convicting those implicated in corruption cases.

Raila's sentiments seemed like an indirect attack on Ruto, who told him (Raila) off.

Ruto stated that the opposition under Raila had run corruption syndicates before and thus had no moral authority to lecture others on dealing with graft.

4. Taifa Leo

According to the publication, Raila will be vying for the country's top seat in the coming vote.

The ODM chief is said to have already started marshalling support for his umpteenth presidential bid.

He is hopeful that his considered last shot will yield results after failing in the previous votes.

Speaking in Mombasa in the past weekend, Raila said his priority would be to revitalise the economy and sentence those involved in corruption.

He had earlier stated that he would announce his bid once the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) process is over.

5. The Standard

The daily reported how parents became cash cows for school heads who charge before issuing Form 1 admission.

According to The Standard, schools charged between KSh 5,000 and KSh 35,000 under the guise of "development fee, " and no admission letter is issued unless paid.

Despite the warning from the government, the school heads claim school boards and parents had approved the charges.
