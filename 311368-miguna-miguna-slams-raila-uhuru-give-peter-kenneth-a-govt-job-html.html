Miguna Miguna slams Raila for asking Uhuru to give Peter Kenneth govt job

July 25, 2024
Post

Controversial Canada-based lawyer Miguna Miguna is a disgusted man after Opposition leader Raila Odinga asked President Uhuru Kenyatta to offer a job to former Gatanga MP Peter Kenneth.

On Thursday, July 25, while addressing mourners during burial of Kenneth's mother Rahab Wambui, Raila praised the ex-presidential candidate as a man of good virtue and urged Uhuru to appoint him in government.

"He (Peter Kenneth) is someone whose integrity is above reproach and he is committed. He is very smart. Mr President, this is someone who needs to be given "something to do". He worked well in the banking sector," said Raila.

In a Twitter post, the vocal legal practitioner said the Opposition leader was not in order to ask for job for a tycoon like Kenneth when there were thousands of unemployed youths languishing in poverty across the country.

"Shame on you, Raila Odinga. Instead of advocating for brilliant young Kenyans like Kelvin Ochieng' to be gainfully employed and empowered, you are begging despot Uhuru to give Peter Kenneth - a billionaire thief who owns an insurance company and a bank - a job? Stop conmanship!," claimed Miguna.

The ODM leader was echoing sentiments earlier made by Murang'a county leaders during the event, including area senator Irungu Kang'ata and Woman Rep Sabina Wanjiru Chege.

They asked Uhuru to offer Kenneth a state job arguing he had a proven track record when he served Gatanga constituency as MP.
