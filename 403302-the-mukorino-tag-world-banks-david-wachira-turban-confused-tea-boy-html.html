David Waigwa Wachira is the World Bank Group's Public Finance and Governance Specialist and Youth-to-Youth (Y2Y) co-secretary.

One thing that stands out about him is the religious turban on his head as he subscribes to the Akorino faith, an African indigenous church associated with strict beliefs and practices.

The perception has for decades led many to believe, wrongly so, that a Mukorino has little or zero chances of rising up the ranks to sit within the highest echelons of power.

It is this tag that has often got David misjudged both in Kenya and across the borders despite holding a PhD in Public Administration & Management from the University of North Texas.

He has also shared tables with global icons like Bill Gates, Michelle Obama, and Hillary Clinton.

The down-to-earth doctor is married to Cecilie, a wedding and portrait photographer in the US. The two live in Washington DC.

In other words, David is a well-educated, well-travelled and exquisite young gentleman flying the Kenyan flag and at very high peaks.

However, his only 'mistake' is being a Mukorino which means wearing a turban.

Abroad, David has often been a victim of labeling, and countless times he has been selected for 'random security checks' at airports.

Weirdly, the same happens back home as fellow Kenyans have a low opinion of him, thanks to the Mukorino tag.

"I had a meeting that I was hosting and I showed up to the meeting promptly. And there was a tea stash at the end and I'm making a cup of tea and a government official walked in and said 'hata mimi nitengenezee chai, so I gave him the one I had," he told Jeff Koinange.

As if that was not enough, David added that another official asked him to make a cup of Milo for him, concluding that he was the tea boy.

It is only when he took his seat as the chairperson that the same people who had concluded he was a tea boy got shocked.

"After the meeting they said 'you know, we need more people like you, that way we would what is going on and they would push our endeavors," he added.

David decried the label that people put of others because they believe a Mukorino cannot go further than a certain level.

"You see your watchman at the gate and you don't care about his humanistic aspect and you put people in these boxes, which is unfortunate," expressed David.
